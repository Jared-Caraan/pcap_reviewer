What is the result of the expression: `10 and 20`?

A. `20`\
B. `10`\
C. `True`\
D. `False`

Answer: A\
Explanation: Since `10` is truthy, the and operator returns the second value.

Which value will be assigned to '`result`' in: `result = 0 or None or [] or 7 or 10`?

A. `0`\
B. `7`\
C. `10`\
D. `None`

Answer: B\
Explanation: The '`or`' operator returns the first truthy value it encounters.
##

In the expression '`not 0 and 5`', which operator is evaluated first?

A. `and`\
B. They are evaluated left-to-right simultaneously\
C. `not`\
D. `0`

Answer: C\
Explanation: In Python, '`not`' has the highest precedence among logical operators.
##

What does the expression '`[] and "Hello"`' return?

A. `[]`\
B. `"Hello"`\
C. `False`\
D. `None`

Answer: A\
Explanation: An empty list is falsy, and the '`and`' operator returns the first falsy value it finds.

What is the final result of: `1 or 5 and 0`?

A. `1`\
B. `True`\
C. `5`\
D. `0`

Answer: A\
Explanation: Python sees '`1`' as truthy in an '`or`' expression and stops immediately.
